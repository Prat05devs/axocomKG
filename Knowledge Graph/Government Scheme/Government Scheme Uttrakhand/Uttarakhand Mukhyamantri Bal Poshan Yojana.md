## Introduction

-   How often do we heard the adage that a healthy body leads to a healthy mind?
-   A nutritious diet is crucial for the development of Healthy body and mind.
-   A well nourished child is better able to focus on their study and sports, which is why a nutritious diet is important for children nowadays.
-   All parents strive hard to provide these nutritional values to their children but...
-   due to financial difficulties some of them are not able to provide this nutritious value.
-   Children are not only the future of their family but also of their state and country.
-   Considering these issues, the Uttarakhand government has started a new scheme called as "Mukhyamantri Bal Poshan Yojana"
-   Under this scheme, the Uttarakhand government has decided to provide basic nutritious support to the children of their state and make a malnutrition free state.
-   As per the scheme, children will be provided with eggs and bananas twice a week to meet their essential nutritional needs.
-   Only such children's are eligible for the 'Mukhyamantri Bal Poshan Yojana' who are in the age group of 3 to 6 years.
-   However, this scheme is restricted to children enrolled in Anganwadi Centers.
-   This means, in order to avail the benefits of 'Mukhyamantri Bal Poshan Yojana,' parents must register their children at an anganwadi centre.
-   These Anganwadi centers are run by the state government.
-   To get the benefits of the scheme, it is important for registered children to be present at the center.
-   Under the scheme, Banana Chips will be provided on Monday and Tuesday, and eggs will be provided on Wednesday and Saturday.
-   For children's who do not eat eggs, bananas will be provided instead.
-   But in case the Bananas are unavailable, any seasonal fruit costs less than Rs 5/- will be provided.
-   A Self Help Group (SHG) or a committee form particularly for this scheme will ensure the availability of the provided items.
-   Additionally, the quality of the items will be ensured by these SHG and committees.
-   For the successful implementation of the scheme, the government has allocated a budget in their 2024-25 budget of 28.47 Cr.

## Benefits of Scheme

-   Under Uttarakhand Mukhyamantri Bal Poshan Abhiyan Yojana, the following benefits will be provided to the beneficiaries: -
    -   Two days in a week an egg and banana chips will be provided.
    -   Bananas will be provided to those who do not eat eggs.
    -   In case of unavailability of bananas, other seasonal fruit that cost around less than Rs 5/- will be given to the children.

## Eligibility Criteria

-   Only beneficiaries who meet the following condition are eligible for the recently announced Mukhyamantri Bal Poshan Yojana: -
    -   Children must be a permanent resident of the state.
    -   Children must be between 3 to 6 years old to get the benefits of the scheme.

## Documents Required

-   The following documents are required to avail the benefit of the Mukhyamantri Bal Poshan Yojana: -
    -   Aadhaar Card.
    -   Birth Certificate.
    -   Guardian Aadhar Card.
    -   Ration Card.
    -   Address Proof.
    -   Income Proof (if required).
    -   Mobile Number.
    -   Children Photograph (or family group photograph).
    -   Caste Certificate.

## How to apply

-   To avail benefit of Uttarakhand Mukhyamantri Bal Poshan Abhiyan Yojana, the beneficiary should be registered in the Anganwadi.
-   To register, the beneficiary need to visit the nearby Anganwadi center.
-   There, they need to collect the registration form from Asha Worker.
-   Now fill up the applicant details carefully.
-   Affix all the required documents along with recent photograph.
-   Once the registration is completed, the benefits of the scheme will be granted by the Anganwadi center.
